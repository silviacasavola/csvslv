TOD
2021
SPECULATIVE DESIGN
↓

"Tod" is a speculative design project developed for my bachelor thesis. Based upon a research on funeral and memorial rituals, the project aims at conveying a reflection on the need of renovating our rituals and adjusting them to modern lifestyle and needs. We speculated on an alternate present in which there is "Tod", a commemoration home device that blends into the environment and the everyday life of its users just like every high-tech appliance. "Tod" guides the user to the correct commemoration of the dead, by suggesting the right frequency and judging the execution.
The two main parts of the project were the physical production and coding of the prototype and the work of fictional branding, that gave form to the whole "Tod" universe, including the brand identity, website, promo videos, social channels and communities.
I curated the website of the brand, which you can visit at www.toditalia.com.

PASTA CHUTE
2021
WEB DESIGN

As harmless as it might look, pasta often ends up at the center of animated debates, especially in our country. "Pasta Chute" is a virtual common space built on p5.js that aims at triggering the same heartfelt involvement, by asking users to root for their favorite type of pasta. The app is playable at: pastachute.herokuapp.com.

The purpose is light and humorous: to entertain people through a very simple, yet engaging experience and to make them feel part of something big, despite (or maybe even thanks to) the trivial subject. The creative process is very similar to the one of memes, as it starts from picking a very simple piece of culture that has a surprisingly big impact because of its universality.

RUEDI BAUR
2019
EDITORIAL DESIGN

The purpose of this project was to design an editorial product, thought as a communication design supplement to the Italian magazine “Multiverso”. Our mag was focused on the work Franco-Swiss designer Ruedi Baur. Since we found his graphic production to be very diverse, we identified his phisolophical attitude as a common thread, rather than a stylistic feature. We were interested in highlighting his conception of design as “integral” (organic, systemic), through a range of different projects and contexts, that we classified on the basis of their focus, either on “nature”, “culture” or the “city”. I took care of the section called “An identity for nature” (“Un’identità per la natura”), dedicated to the work of Ruedi Baur on territories and natural landscapes. The title was chosen for its ambiguous meaning. The two words, “identity” and “difference”, might be interpreted as antithetical, for their meaning of ”similarity” and “dissimilarity”; but “identity” is also the technical term that defines what Baur designs. In order to do so, he says, it’s necessary to create “differences”, individuality.

2018
PHOTOGRAPHY
